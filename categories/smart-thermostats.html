Smart Thermostat Rebates & Incentives

Smart thermostats help reduce heating and cooling costs by learning your schedule and optimizing temperature settings automatically. While the federal tax credit for thermostats is modest, many utilities offer instant rebates or free smart thermostats to their customers.

Federal programs for Smart Thermostats

25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

Up to $150 for qualifying ENERGY STAR smart thermostats. Part of the $1,200 annual cap for non-heat-pump improvements.

Eligibility: Existing homes only. The thermostat must be ENERGY STAR certified.

Frequently asked questions

Do utilities offer free smart thermostats?

Many utilities offer free or heavily discounted ENERGY STAR smart thermostats as part of demand response or energy savings programs. Check with your local utility or enter your ZIP code to find available offers.

How much can a smart thermostat save on energy bills?

ENERGY STAR estimates that a smart thermostat can save an average of 8% on heating and cooling bills, or roughly $50 per year depending on your climate and usage.

Which smart thermostats qualify for rebates?

ENERGY STAR certified models from brands like Ecobee, Google Nest, and Honeywell typically qualify for federal credits and utility rebates.
