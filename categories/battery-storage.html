Battery Storage Rebates & Incentives

Home battery storage systems provide backup power during outages, help you maximize solar self-consumption, and can reduce peak electricity charges. The federal tax credit now covers standalone batteries, making energy storage more accessible than ever.

Federal programs for Battery Storage

25D Residential Clean Energy Credit

30% of the total cost of a battery storage system (must have at least 3 kWh capacity). No annual cap. Standalone batteries qualify as of 2023.

Eligibility: Must be a home you own in the US. Battery must have a capacity of at least 3 kWh. Does not need to be paired with solar.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need solar panels to get the battery tax credit?

No. As of 2023, standalone battery storage systems qualify for the 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit independently of solar panels.

How much does a home battery system cost?

Typical home battery systems cost $10,000–$20,000 installed. After the 30% federal credit, your net cost would be $7,000–$14,000.

Are there state incentives for batteries?

Several states (California, Massachusetts, Oregon, and others) offer additional battery storage rebates ranging from $1,000 to $5,000 or more.
