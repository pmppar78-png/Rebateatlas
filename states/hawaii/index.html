Energy Rebates in Hawaii

Hawaii residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Hawaii, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Hawaii

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Hawaii Energy Rebate Overview

Hawaii households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Hawaii has launched both the HOMES (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings) and HEAR (High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate) programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, making additional point-of-sale rebates available to qualifying residents. The Hawaii State Energy Office coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Hawaii

Hawaii has launched both the HOMES and HEAR programs through the Hawaii State Energy Office, providing rebates for whole-home energy efficiency retrofits and high-efficiency electric appliances. Hawaii received over $48 million in IRA funding for these programs.

Income Eligibility for Hawaii Rebates

For a family of four in Honolulu County, 80% AMI is approximately $86,400. Outer islands have lower thresholds. Low-income households qualify for up to 100% of project costs covered under HOMES.

For a family of four in Honolulu County, 150% AMI is approximately $162,000. Moderate-income households qualify for up to 50% of project costs under HOMES.

Hawaii State Tax Credits

Hawaii Renewable Energy Technologies Income Tax Credit

Amount: 35% of installed cost for solar PV (capped at $5,000), 35% for solar thermal (capped at $2,250), and 20% for wind systems (capped at $1,500)

Eligible items: Solar photovoltaic systems, solar thermal systems, wind energy systems, and battery storage systems paired with renewable energy

This is a non-refundable state income tax credit that can be carried forward. The credit is claimed in the tax year the system is placed in service. It stacks with the federal 30% ITC.

Hawaii State Rebate Programs

Hawaii Energy Efficiency Rebate Program

Administered by: Hawaii Energy (administered by Leidos)

Amount: $200-$1,500 depending on measure

Eligible items: Energy-efficient appliances, LED lighting, HVAC systems, heat pump water heaters, building envelope improvements

Hawaii Energy is the state's ratepayer-funded conservation and efficiency program. Rebates are available for residential and commercial customers of HECO, MECO, and HELCO.

Learn more

Heat Pump Water Heater Rebate

Administered by: Hawaii Energy

Amount: Up to $1,000 for qualifying heat pump water heaters

Eligible items: ENERGY STAR certified heat pump water heaters replacing electric resistance or propane water heaters

Additional bonuses may be available for income-qualified households. Must be installed by a licensed contractor.

Solar Net Metering in Hawaii

Hawaii transitioned from traditional net metering to Customer Grid-Supply (CGS) and Customer Self-Supply (CSS) programs. Under CGS, customers receive a credit for exported solar energy at a rate lower than the retail rate. CSS requires all solar generation to be used on-site. Smart Export programs offer time-of-use export credits.
