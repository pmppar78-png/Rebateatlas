Energy Rebates in Minnesota

Minnesota residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Minnesota, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Minnesota

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Minnesota Energy Rebate Overview

Minnesota households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Minnesota has launched both the HOMES (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings) and HEAR (High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate) programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, making additional point-of-sale rebates available to qualifying residents. The Minnesota Department of Commerce - Division of Energy Resources coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Minnesota

Minnesota has launched HOMES and HEAR programs through the Minnesota Department of Commerce. The state received approximately $80 million in IRA funding for energy efficiency and electrification rebates, complementing existing Conservation Improvement Programs (CIP) run by utilities.

Income Eligibility for Minnesota Rebates

For a family of four in the Minneapolis-St. Paul metro area, 80% AMI is approximately $80,200. Low-income households qualify for up to 100% of project costs under HOMES and for enhanced utility CIP programs.

For a family of four in the Minneapolis-St. Paul metro area, 150% AMI is approximately $150,375. Moderate-income households qualify for rebates covering up to 50% of project costs under HOMES.

Minnesota State Tax Credits

Minnesota Solar Energy Sales Tax Exemption

Amount: 100% exemption from state sales tax on solar energy systems

Eligible items: Solar PV systems, solar thermal systems, and associated equipment

Minnesota exempts solar energy equipment from the state sales tax (6.875%) and local sales taxes. This effectively reduces the cost of a solar installation by the sales tax amount. No application required; the exemption is applied at point of sale.

Learn more

Minnesota Solar Energy Production Incentive (Xcel Energy)

Amount: Performance-based incentive of approximately $0.08/kWh for 10 years (program now has a waitlist/limited availability)

Eligible items: Solar PV systems installed by Xcel Energy customers in Minnesota

Xcel Energy's Solar*Rewards program provides production-based incentives. The program has been very popular and may have limited availability or waitlists. Check with Xcel Energy for current program status and rates.

Minnesota State Rebate Programs

Minnesota Conservation Improvement Programs (CIP)

Administered by: All Minnesota utilities under Department of Commerce oversight

Amount: Varies by utility: $50-$3,000+ depending on measure

Eligible items: Insulation, air sealing, HVAC systems, heat pumps, smart thermostats, ENERGY STAR appliances, LED lighting, water heaters

Minnesota law requires utilities to invest at least 1.5% of gross operating revenue in energy conservation programs. Each utility offers its own suite of rebates and programs. This is one of the most comprehensive statewide efficiency program mandates in the country.

Learn more

Minnesota Weatherization Assistance Program

Administered by: Minnesota Department of Commerce / Community Action Agencies

Amount: Average of $8,000+ per home

Eligible items: Insulation, air sealing, furnace repair and replacement, water heater replacement, health and safety improvements

Available to households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. Minnesota's program is among the most active in the nation given the cold climate.

Learn more

Minnesota Fix-Up Fund / Home Energy Loans

Administered by: Minnesota Housing Finance Agency

Amount: Low-interest loans up to $50,000 for home improvements including energy efficiency

Eligible items: Energy efficiency improvements, HVAC systems, windows, insulation, solar PV, and other home improvements

Below-market interest rate loans available through participating lenders. Income limits apply. Can be combined with utility rebates.

Learn more

Solar Net Metering in Minnesota

Minnesota requires all utilities to offer net metering for systems up to 1 MW. Customers receive credit at the retail rate for excess generation. Minnesota also has a strong community solar garden program, particularly through Xcel Energy, allowing subscribers to receive bill credits for their share of a community solar project.

Frequently Asked Questions About Minnesota Rebates

State-level programs include Minnesota Conservation Improvement Programs (CIP), Minnesota Weatherization Assistance Program, Minnesota Fix-Up Fund / Home Energy Loans. Minnesota has also launched the federal HOMES and HEAR rebate programs for income-qualifying households.

How do I apply for energy rebates in Minnesota?
