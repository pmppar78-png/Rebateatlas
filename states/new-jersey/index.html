Energy Rebates in New Jersey

New Jersey residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in New Jersey, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in New Jersey

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

New Jersey Energy Rebate Overview

New Jersey households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. New Jersey has launched both the HOMES (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings) and HEAR (High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate) programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, making additional point-of-sale rebates available to qualifying residents. The New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU) coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in New Jersey

New Jersey has launched its HOMES and HEAR rebate programs under the Inflation Reduction Act. The New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU) is administering the programs in coordination with utility-run efficiency programs and the state's clean energy initiative.

Income Eligibility for New Jersey Rebates

For New Jersey, 80% of Area Median Income varies by county. In Essex County (Newark), 80% AMI for a family of four is approximately $79,900. In Bergen County, it is approximately $88,650.

For New Jersey, 150% of Area Median Income varies by county. In Essex County (Newark), 150% AMI for a family of four is approximately $149,800. In Bergen County, it is approximately $166,200.

New Jersey State Tax Credits

New Jersey Sales Tax Exemption for Solar

Amount: 100% sales tax exemption on solar energy system equipment

Eligible items: Solar photovoltaic systems, solar energy equipment and components

New Jersey exempts solar energy system equipment from the state sales tax, reducing the upfront cost of installation.

New Jersey Property Tax Exemption for Renewable Energy

Amount: 100% property tax exemption for the added value of renewable energy systems

Eligible items: Solar panels, wind energy systems, and other renewable energy installations

The added value of a renewable energy system is exempt from local property taxes in New Jersey.

New Jersey State Rebate Programs

NJ Clean Energy Program - Residential New Construction and HVAC

Administered by: New Jersey Board of Public Utilities / TRC (program administrator)

Amount: Up to $1,000 for heat pumps, $500-$1,000 for HVAC upgrades, $250-$500 for insulation

Eligible items: High-efficiency HVAC systems, heat pumps, insulation, air sealing, duct sealing, ENERGY STAR windows

The NJ Clean Energy Program provides rebates for residential energy efficiency improvements funded through the Societal Benefits Charge on utility bills.

Learn more

Successor Solar Incentive Program (SuSI) / ADI

Administered by: New Jersey Board of Public Utilities

Amount: Fixed incentive payments per kWh of solar generation for 15 years (Administratively Determined Incentive)

Eligible items: Residential rooftop solar photovoltaic systems

The ADI program replaced the legacy SREC program and SREC-II transitional program. It provides a fixed, long-term incentive payment for solar energy generation.

Learn more

Whole-House Pilot Program

Administered by: NJ Clean Energy Program

Amount: Up to $4,000 for moderate-income, up to $8,000 for low-income households for comprehensive improvements

Eligible items: Comprehensive whole-home energy efficiency improvements including insulation, air sealing, HVAC, and water heaters

Pilot program providing enhanced incentives for whole-house energy efficiency improvements, with higher rebates available for income-qualifying households.

Learn more

Solar Net Metering in New Jersey

New Jersey has strong net metering rules. Residential customers can net meter systems up to 10 MW. Customers receive a full retail rate credit for excess generation, carried forward monthly and trued up annually. New Jersey also has a successor solar incentive program (SREC-II/ADI) that provides additional compensation.
