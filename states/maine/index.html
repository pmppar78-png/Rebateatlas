Energy Rebates in Maine

Maine residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Maine, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Maine

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Maine Energy Rebate Overview

Maine households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Maine has launched both the HOMES (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings) and HEAR (High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate) programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, making additional point-of-sale rebates available to qualifying residents. The Governor's Energy Office coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Maine

Maine has launched both HOMES and HEAR programs through Efficiency Maine Trust. The state received approximately $52 million in IRA funding. Efficiency Maine is administering rebates for whole-home energy retrofits and high-efficiency electric appliances including heat pumps.

Income Eligibility for Maine Rebates

For a family of four in Portland, 80% AMI is approximately $65,650. Low-income households qualify for up to 100% of project costs under HOMES, with enhanced rebates through Efficiency Maine.

For a family of four in Portland, 150% AMI is approximately $123,100. Moderate-income households qualify for rebates covering up to 50% of project costs under HOMES.

Maine State Rebate Programs

Efficiency Maine Heat Pump Program

Administered by: Efficiency Maine Trust

Amount: $800 per indoor unit for standard rebate; up to $2,400 per heat pump for income-eligible households (up to 3 units)

Eligible items: Ductless mini-split heat pumps, ducted heat pumps, heat pump water heaters that meet Efficiency Maine efficiency requirements

One of the most successful heat pump rebate programs in the nation. Income-eligible customers (at or below 80% AMI) receive enhanced rebates. Must use a registered Efficiency Maine vendor for installation.

Learn more

Efficiency Maine Home Insulation Program

Administered by: Efficiency Maine Trust

Amount: Up to $8,000 rebate for air sealing and insulation; up to $12,000 for income-eligible households

Eligible items: Attic insulation, wall insulation, basement/crawl space insulation, air sealing, blower door testing

Requires a pre and post energy assessment. Must use an Efficiency Maine registered contractor. Income-eligible households receive significantly higher rebate amounts.

Learn more

Efficiency Maine Heat Pump Water Heater Program

Administered by: Efficiency Maine Trust

Amount: $950 rebate for standard; $1,500 for income-eligible households

Eligible items: ENERGY STAR certified heat pump water heaters replacing electric resistance, oil, or propane water heaters

Available at point of sale through participating distributors or as a mail-in rebate. Must meet minimum efficiency requirements.

Learn more

Efficiency Maine EV Rebate Program

Administered by: Efficiency Maine Trust

Amount: $2,000 for new BEVs, $1,000 for new PHEVs; enhanced rebates for income-eligible

Eligible items: New battery electric vehicles, plug-in hybrid electric vehicles, and used EVs

Additional rebates available for income-qualified households. Maine is working to electrify both homes and transportation.

Learn more

Solar Net Metering in Maine

Maine enacted LD 1711 in 2019 creating a net energy billing (NEB) program that replaced traditional net metering. Customers receive bill credits at a rate based on the standard offer rate for excess generation. Systems up to 660 kW qualify. Community solar projects and third-party ownership are permitted. Credits can be used to offset delivery charges.

Frequently Asked Questions About Maine Rebates

Maine residents can access federal tax credits including the 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (up to $3,200/year) and the 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit (30% for solar and battery storage). State-level programs include Efficiency Maine Heat Pump Program, Efficiency Maine Home Insulation Program, Efficiency Maine Heat Pump Water Heater Program, Efficiency Maine EV Rebate Program. Maine has also launched the federal HOMES and HEAR rebate programs for income-qualifying households.
