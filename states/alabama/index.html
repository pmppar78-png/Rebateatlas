Energy Rebates & Incentives in Alabama

Alabama sits in a warm, humid climate zone where air conditioning dominates household energy bills for much of the year. The state draws its electricity from a mix of natural gas, coal, nuclear, and a growing share of renewables. For Alabama homeowners, federal tax credits and rebate programs represent the most significant opportunities to lower the cost of energy-efficient upgrades, while the Tennessee Valley Authority and Alabama Power provide additional utility-level incentives worth exploring.

HOMES & HEAR Rebates in Alabama

Under the Inflation Reduction Act, two major rebate programs are headed to every state: the Home Efficiency Rebates (HOMES) program and the Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates (HEAR) program. In Alabama, the designated administrator is the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs (ADECA). As of early 2026, Alabama has not yet launched either program and remains in the planning and application phase with the U.S. Department of Energy. Once these programs go live, income-qualifying Alabama households could receive point-of-sale rebates of up to $8,000 for heat pumps and up to $14,000 in total electrification rebates through HEAR, while the HOMES program will reward whole-home energy savings with rebates up to $8,000 for low-income households. Federal Tax Credits for Alabama Homeowners

Regardless of the HOMES and HEAR timeline, Alabama residents can claim these federal tax credits right now on qualifying improvements:

  • 25C Home Improvement Credit: Claim up to $3,200 per year for efficiency upgrades. This includes up to $2,000 for a qualifying heat pump or heat pump water heater, plus up to $1,200 for insulation, windows, doors, and electrical panel upgrades. In Alabama's hot summers, upgrading to a high-efficiency heat pump can dramatically cut cooling costs while qualifying for this credit.
  • 25D Clean Energy Credit: Receive a 30% tax credit with no annual cap on solar panel installations, battery storage systems, and geothermal heat pumps. Alabama averages around 200 sunny days per year, making residential solar a viable investment. Compare quotes from installers through a marketplace like EnergySage to find the best deal for your roof.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Homeowners in eligible census tracts can claim up to $1,000 (30% of cost) for installing a home EV charger. Many rural and low-income areas in Alabama qualify. If you are considering a home charging station, ChargePoint offers popular Level 2 home chargers.

Alabama State Rebate Programs

Alabama does not currently operate a large state-funded rebate program for residential energy efficiency, but two key programs serve residents across the state:

TVA EnergyRight Residential Services -- Administered by the Tennessee Valley Authority and its local power company distributors, this program is available to customers in northern Alabama served by utilities such as Huntsville Utilities, Decatur Utilities, and others. Rebates of up to $1,500 are available for qualifying heat pumps, along with incentives for insulation, air sealing, duct sealing, and ENERGY STAR appliances. The program also includes free home energy evaluations to help homeowners identify the most impactful improvements. If you need help finding a qualified HVAC contractor, consider getting estimates through Thumbtack.

Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) -- Administered by ADECA through local community action agencies, this federally funded program provides an average of approximately $7,669 per home in energy-saving improvements at no cost to qualifying households. Eligible measures include insulation, air sealing, heating and cooling system repair or replacement, and window and door improvements. Households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level are eligible to apply.

State Tax Credits & Incentives

Alabama does not currently offer additional state-level tax credits for residential energy efficiency or renewable energy beyond the federal credits listed above. This makes the federal 25C, 25D, and 30C credits especially important for Alabama homeowners looking to offset upgrade costs. Net Metering & Solar Policy in Alabama

Alabama does not have a statewide net metering policy, which is an important consideration for homeowners evaluating rooftop solar. Alabama Power offers a rate-based program called Rate Rider RCG for small renewable generators up to 25 kW, but it credits customers at the avoided cost rate rather than the full retail rate. This means the payback period for solar in Alabama is longer than in states with full retail-rate net metering. Pairing solar panels with a battery storage system can help maximize self-consumption and reduce reliance on grid exports. The federal 25D credit applies to both the solar panels and the battery, helping offset the combined cost.

Utility Rebate Programs

Alabama's largest utilities offer their own efficiency incentive programs. Here is what is currently available:

Alabama Power

  • Residential Energy Checkup: A free home energy assessment that identifies efficiency improvement opportunities and helps prioritize upgrades with the highest return on investment.
  • Appliance Rebate Program: Rebates ranging from $50 to $300 on ENERGY STAR certified heat pumps, water heaters, and smart thermostats.

Huntsville Utilities

  • TVA EnergyRight Heat Pump Rebate: Up to $1,500 for ENERGY STAR certified heat pumps that meet minimum efficiency requirements. This is one of the most generous utility-level heat pump rebates available in the state.

Riviera Utilities

  • TVA EnergyRight Residential Rebates: Rebates vary by measure and cover heat pumps, insulation, and duct sealing for customers in the Riviera Utilities service area in southern Alabama.

Income Eligibility in Alabama

Several Alabama programs, including the upcoming HOMES and HEAR rebates, offer enhanced benefits based on household income. For the IRA rebate programs, the key thresholds are 80% and 150% of Area Median Income (AMI). In Alabama, these figures vary by county. For example, in Jefferson County (Birmingham), 80% AMI for a family of four is approximately $55,300, while 150% AMI is approximately $103,700. Rural counties may have significantly lower thresholds. Households at or below 80% AMI are classified as low-income and qualify for the largest rebate amounts, while those between 80% and 150% AMI are considered moderate-income and still receive substantial support. The Weatherization Assistance Program uses a separate threshold of 200% of the federal poverty level.
