Energy Rebates in Indiana

Indiana residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Indiana, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Indiana

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Indiana Energy Rebate Overview

Indiana households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Indiana has not yet launched the HOMES and HEAR rebate programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, but federal tax credits are available now to all homeowners regardless of state program status. The Indiana Office of Energy Development coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Indiana

Indiana's HOMES and HEAR programs are pending launch. The Indiana Office of Energy Development is working on program design and implementation using approximately $84 million in IRA funding allocated to Indiana.

Income Eligibility for Indiana Rebates

For a family of four in Indianapolis, 80% AMI is approximately $60,100. Low-income households will qualify for the highest rebate levels under HOMES when the program launches.

For a family of four in Indianapolis, 150% AMI is approximately $112,700. Moderate-income households will qualify for partial rebates under HOMES when the program launches.

Indiana Weatherization Assistance Program

Administered by: Indiana Housing and Community Development Authority (IHCDA)

Amount: Average of $7,669 per home

Eligible items: Insulation, air sealing, furnace repair or replacement, water heater replacement, window and door repair

Available to households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. Priority given to elderly, disabled, and families with young children.

Energizing Indiana (Successor Programs)

Administered by: Individual utilities under IURC oversight

Amount: Varies by utility and measure

Eligible items: Energy efficiency upgrades including HVAC, insulation, and appliances through individual utility programs

Following the end of the statewide Energizing Indiana program, individual utilities now administer their own demand-side management programs under Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission oversight.

Solar Net Metering in Indiana

Indiana's net metering policy was phased down under SEA 309 (2017). Customers who installed solar before July 2022 retain full retail-rate net metering for 30 years. New installations receive a gradually declining excess distributed generation (EDG) credit rate, trending toward the utility's avoided cost rate by 2032.
