Energy Rebates in Iowa

Iowa residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Iowa, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Iowa

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Iowa Energy Rebate Overview

Iowa households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Iowa has not yet launched the HOMES and HEAR rebate programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, but federal tax credits are available now to all homeowners regardless of state program status. The Iowa Economic Development Authority - Energy Office coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Iowa

Iowa's HOMES and HEAR programs are pending launch. The Iowa Economic Development Authority is developing program plans and implementation timelines using approximately $45 million in IRA funding allocated to the state.

Income Eligibility for Iowa Rebates

For a family of four in Des Moines, 80% AMI is approximately $62,400. Low-income households will qualify for the highest rebate levels under HOMES when the program launches.

For a family of four in Des Moines, 150% AMI is approximately $117,000. Moderate-income households will qualify for partial rebates under HOMES when the program launches.

Iowa State Tax Credits

Iowa Solar Energy System Tax Credit

Amount: 15% of the federal solar tax credit amount (effectively 4.5% of installed cost), capped at $5,000 for residential

Eligible items: Solar PV and solar thermal systems installed on residential or commercial properties in Iowa

The credit is refundable and claimed in the year the system is placed in service. Must be installed by a certified installer. Applications must be submitted before installation begins. The program has an annual cap, so credits are awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.

Iowa State Rebate Programs

Iowa Weatherization Assistance Program

Administered by: Iowa Economic Development Authority / Local Community Action Agencies

Amount: Average of $7,500+ per home

Eligible items: Air sealing, insulation, furnace repair and replacement, water heater replacement, window and door repairs

Available to households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. No cost to qualifying residents.

Solar Net Metering in Iowa

Iowa requires investor-owned utilities to offer net metering under the state's alternate energy production (AEP) facility rules. Systems up to 500 kW qualify. Excess generation is credited at the utility's avoided cost rate. Municipal and cooperative utilities may have different policies.
