Energy Rebates in Idaho

Idaho residents can access a range of federal energy tax credits, state-specific incentive programs, and local utility rebates to help offset the cost of energy-efficient home improvements. This page covers the key programs available in Idaho, including heat pump rebates, solar incentives, EV charger credits, weatherization assistance, and more. Each program listed below includes eligibility details and links to official sources.

Federal Programs Available in Idaho

  • 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: Up to $3,200/year for heat pumps, insulation, windows, doors, electrical panels, and home energy audits. Covers 30% of costs.
  • 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit: 30% tax credit for solar panels and battery storage with no dollar cap. Available through 2032.
  • HOMES & HEAR Rebates: Income-qualified point-of-sale rebates up to $14,000 for heat pumps, water heaters, electrical panels, and other electrification upgrades.
  • 30C EV Charger Credit: Up to $1,000 for home EV charging equipment in eligible census tracts.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services for low-income households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Idaho Energy Rebate Overview

Idaho households have access to a combination of federal, state, and local utility incentives designed to make energy-efficient home upgrades more affordable. Idaho has not yet launched the HOMES and HEAR rebate programs under the Inflation Reduction Act, but federal tax credits are available now to all homeowners regardless of state program status. The Idaho Office of Energy and Mineral Resources coordinates state-level energy programs and can provide the latest information on available incentives.

HOMES & HEAR Program Status in Idaho

Idaho's HOMES and HEAR programs are pending launch. The Idaho Office of Energy and Mineral Resources is developing program guidelines and implementation plans using approximately $30 million in IRA funding allocated to the state.

Income Eligibility for Idaho Rebates

For a family of four in Boise, 80% AMI is approximately $58,250. Low-income households will qualify for the highest rebate levels under HOMES when launched.

For a family of four in Boise, 150% AMI is approximately $109,200. Moderate-income households will qualify for partial rebates under HOMES when launched.

Idaho State Tax Credits

Idaho Residential Alternative Energy Tax Deduction

Amount: 40% of cost in year one (up to $5,000), then 20% per year for the next three years (up to $5,000 total deduction)

Eligible items: Solar PV, solar thermal, geothermal, wind, and fuel cell systems for residential properties

This is a state income tax deduction, not a credit. The total deduction cannot exceed $20,000 over four years. Must be for the taxpayer's primary residence in Idaho.

Idaho State Rebate Programs

Idaho Weatherization Assistance Program

Administered by: Idaho Community Action Network

Amount: Up to $8,009 average per home

Eligible items: Insulation, air sealing, furnace repair or replacement, window repair, and other weatherization measures

Federally funded program available to low-income households. Income must be at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Solar Net Metering in Idaho

Idaho offers net metering for systems up to 100 kW for residential customers. Customers receive a credit at the utility's avoided cost rate for excess generation, which is lower than the retail rate. Credits carry over monthly and are reconciled annually.
